PlayStation 3

controller

The PlayStation 3, or PS3, was launched by Sony in 2006. It was notable for its advanced processing power, featuring the Cell processor, and for being Sony’s first console with high-definition graphics. The PS3 supported Blu-ray discs, offering a new level of detail and interactivity in games and media. It also marked the debut of the PlayStation Network (PSN), allowing online multiplayer gaming, digital downloads, and multimedia streaming. Popular titles for the PS3 included The Last of Us, Uncharted, and God of War III, and it was backward compatible with many PS2 games.

PlayStation 2

controller

The PlayStation 2, commonly referred to as PS2, was released by Sony in 2000. It became one of the best-selling video game consoles of all time due to its massive game library, innovative graphics, and multimedia capabilities. The PS2 supported DVD playback, which made it not only a gaming console but also a popular entertainment device for families. It introduced many iconic games and franchises, such as Grand Theft Auto, Metal Gear Solid, and Final Fantasy. Its compatibility with original PlayStation (PS1) games further boosted its popularity and longevity.
